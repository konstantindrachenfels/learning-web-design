Create your personalised image classifier

You are sick of the convolutional neural networks (CNN) out there?

Wait, you are probably asking yourself now: "What the heck is a convolutional neural network?". Well, the CNNs are the tools you use to automatically analyze, classify and label images. If you wanna look at a more in-depth definition click here! So, CNNs are a pretty great thing. They make the benefits of machine learning in the area of image recognition useable for everyone. In fact, there are some out there, like MobileNet , who are really fast and easy to use. If you rather want more sophisticated outputs and are thus willing to use a larger, slower and more complex CNNs you might want to have a look at some other solutions like GoogLeNet.

So, why should you be sick of them if they are so great???

Well, once you take a closer look you will find out that these CNNs are only as good as the image libraries they use. Let us take the userfriendly MobileNet for example. MobileNet uses the images from ImageNet to train it's algorithm. This is great as ImageNet includes more than 14 million images! Nonetheless, ImageNet is not a professional database designed to represent everything there is. This means that it includes a lot of pictures of Chihuahuas as they are cute and adorable and hence you will have a high likelihood of MobileNet to correctly recognize Chihuahuas. If you rather want MobileNet to recognize something rare and special like a Quokka , it will not be able to recognize these adorable creatures as it has not been trained to do so. This is so as there has not been created a category for Quokkas in ImageNet. There exist a little more than 20.000 categories in ImageNet. Humans are for example also not categorized. Thus, MobileNet will not be able to recognize you or any other person.

Train MobileNet to recognize something unique!

You might be looking into this whole image recognition area because you want the CNN to recognize something special like your friends, favorite stuffed animals or whatsoever. If that wasn't the reason that you looked into CNNs, well then you might be pumped to try this out now! But how do you do that? There has been offered a really easy and user friendly way to do so using ml5. So ml5 is this awesome open source library developed by NYU professors and many more developers from all over the world. I really strongly encourage you to check out their website. The best way to figre out what you all can do with ml5 is to browse through the examples on the website or to check out the youtube tutorials from one of the developers, Daniel Shiffman.
